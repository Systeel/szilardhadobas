Studies
   I started my studies in my home city, Eger, Hungary in the Balint Balassi elementary school. At the age of ten after a successful mathematics test I learnt that there are machines called 'computer' because I was moved to an experimental computer science class, and started to learn programming theory, Commodore basic, and later Turbo Pascal languages accompanied with strong mathematics and physics. Also due to the computers the whole class switched from russian language classes (which was usual back in the days) to english.
   With some luck, I managed to land in one of the best high schools of Eger, the Neumann Janos High which was run by World Bank Group. We started to learn videography there on an afterschool class and shot and edited our first films in VHS. After a general two years I specialized in natural sciences and started to play with 3D programs like Imagine 2, Vistapro, Lightwave and rendered my first CG images. Also experimented with sound design and game modding besides studies.
   In 1998 I was accepted to the famous Eötvös Lóránd Science University in Budapest, Hungary, and started my rough studies in physics. I also started to take CG image generation more serious and attended different 3dsmax courses and started to use the software for creating more advanced images and early animations as well as modeling, texturing to games and visualizing scientific results like the book covers and images of Chaotic Dynamics book. I also enrolled to Computer Sciences studies and besides school I learnt how to program in Maxscript. In a student science project I created my most complex program in Pascal, the 4d Studio which is a 4 dimensional model editor and used it to study properties of polytops. Later I had a chance to attend Klagenfurt University for one semester with CEEPUS program and spent a summer in the USA with a work and travel program and had a chance to travel through the whole country. I became mesmerized in the world of microbiology so I chose the biological physics specialization. In 2008 I finally finished physics MSc studies and submitted my thesis titled 'Developing advanced methods in Electrontomography' where I used 3dsmax to reduce the noise created by the imperfections of the microscope mechanics. One year later I successfully finished my studies in Computer Sciences. My thesis was a procedural animation system in real-time environment written in C#.
   In 2012 I decided to take a big leap in my animation career and enroller to Animationmentor.com and besides daytime job(s) I managed to finish it gaining crazy amount of animation knowledge. I travelled to the US again to attend Siggraph and the graduation ceremony. Few years later I developed my animation skills further by attending the Animals and Creatures special class which was so successful that two of my shots made it to the school's demo reel.
   As I started to work in the global film industry I felt the need to extend my skills with acting to be able to tackle more complex shots. At Framestore I was very lucky to be able to attend Bruce MacKinnon's acting for animators course with a small group of animators and learnt improvisation techniques which later came very useful when I worked on feature animation.
   In Montreal I had an opportunity to personally attend the masterclass of the legendary Pixar animator, Michal Makarewicz. I deepened my knowledge with techniqes of advanced polishing and methods of layered and mastercurve-based workflows.

Personal

Project Vera (2019-)

   During my time in the film and VFX industry, I experienced that production departments might lack a tool which helps optimize the project. I believe as humans are a visual creatures it might be helpful to visualize the dependencies and make that the main interface for the project management rather than sheets. Over the time of the prototype development, I also added automatization and optimization features so now this experimental tool has the capability of designing the project, bidding and controlling the production throughout the whole process. With the growing importance of resource management and optimization in productions, I believe tools like this will become increasingly universal in the future.

Bit (2010-)

   I'm passionate about exploring the microscopic world, so I decided to embark on a short film project that depicts a character inhabiting an abstract world inside a computer. The film captures his adventures as he navigates through various devices. After several years of working on it in the afternoons, with the help of professional artists, I finally have a script, storyboard, character and environment concepts. I'm hopeful that, in the future, I will be able to bring this story to life.

Maze generator (2017)

   Back in 2017 I started a new life in London UK. In the first few months before I managed to build contacts I spent my free time building this maze generator in 3dsmax Maxscript. This is a basic concept for creating labyrinths out of an image input where the user can paint start and endpoints, and different parameters and the algorithm ensures that there is only a required number of possible routes in the result. In the long term, this tool might be used for automatic level generation in the game industry.

Crawl Engine (2020)

   During the outbreak of Covid, I was working on a project with insects and at home I was exploring ways to speed up the animation process for these multi-legged creatures. To achieve this, I developed a real-time system in Maya that can be integrated into any IK system and used to control simple limbs, such as insect legs. Unfortunately, due to the pandemic, the project was postponed and I had to stop the development of the tool. However, the system is still available for use if required.

Tools/Downloads

Selection Palette (2006-2015)

   I am a picker enthusiast, because it speeds up work extremely. But only a good one can do that. The original idea behind this picker (from even before I knew that other pickers are existing) is the dynamism. Separating the creating and picking mode was never on the table, instead any user can modify the layout any time adding and removing selection sets by a click of a button. Other unique feature of the tool is the 'draw selection' which makes navigating and selecting more complex patterns easy and fast. It is available to download for 3dsmax and the tutorial can be found by clicking the image to the right.

Youtube safegrab

   Animators use references from different online video platforms all the time. Footages need to be downloaded so they can be converted and seen frame by frame in the animation software. In most of the time the downloading sites are free but dodgy and full of adverts which makes acquiring the reference exhausting also this procedure might cause a safety risk. With all of these in my mind I wrote my own Youtube downloader to avoid those annoying artifacts. It is available freely for anyone in the industry but needs authentication to avoid unwanted visitors. If you would find it useful, send me a message on the top of this site and I can give you access. Also you can access the tool clicking on the image.
