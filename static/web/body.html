   As an experienced animator with a strong technical background, I am passionate about bringing characters to life through my work. Studies
   I started my studies in my home city, Eger, Hungary in the Balint Balassi elementary school. At the age of ten after a successful mathematics test I learnt that there are machines called 'computer' because I was moved to an experimental computer science class, and started to learn programming theory, Commodore basic, and later Turbo Pascal languages accompanied with strong mathematics and physics. Also due to the computers the whole class switched from russian language classes (which was usual back in the days) to english.
   With some luck, I managed to land in one of the best high schools of Eger, the Neumann Janos High which was run by World Bank Group. We started to learn videography there on an afterschool class and shot and edited our first films in VHS. After a general two years I specialized in natural sciences and started to play with 3D programs like Imagine 2, Vistapro, Lightwave. Also experimented with sound design and game modding besides studies.
   In 1998 I was accepted to the famous Eötvös Lóránd Science University in Budapest, Hungary, and started my rough studies in physics. I also started to take CG image generation more serious and attended different 3dsmax courses and started to use the software for modeling, texturing to games and visualizing scientific results like the book covers and images of Chaotic Dynamics book. I also enrolled to Computer Sciences studies and besides school I learnt how to program in Maxscript. In a student science project I created my most complex program in Pascal, the 4d Studio which is a 4 dimensional model editor and used it to study properties of polytops. Later I had a chance to attend Klagenfurt University for one semester with CEEPUS program and spent a summer in the USA with a work and travel program and had a chance to travel through the whole country. I became mesmerized in the world of microbiology so I chose the biological physics specialization. In 2008 I finally finished physics MsC studies and submitted my thesis titled 'Developing advanced methods in Electrontomography' where I used 3dsmax to reduce the noise created by the imperfections of the microscope mechanics. One year later I successfully finished my studies in Computer Sciences. My thesis was a procedural animation system in real-time environment written in C#.
   In 2012 I decided to take a big leap in my animation career and enroller to Animationmentor.com and besides daytime job(s) I managed to finish it gaining crazy amount of animation knowledge. I travelled to the US again to attend Siggraph and the graduation ceremony. Few years later I developed my animation skills further by attending the Animals and Creatures special class which was so successful that two of my shots made it to the school's demo reel.
   As I started to work in the global film industry I felt the need to extend my skills with acting to be able to tackle more complex shots. At Framestore I was very lucky to be able to attend Bruce MacKinnon's acting for animators course with a small group of animators and learnt improvisation techniques which later came very useful when I worked on feature animation.
   In Montreal I had an opportunity to personally attend the masterclass of the legendary Pixar animator, Michal Makarewicz. I deepened my knowledge with techniqes of advanced polishing and methods of layered and mastercurve-based workflows.
